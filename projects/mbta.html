GBH Impact and Equity: Boston Bus Equity

Team: Youran Geng, Huihao Xing, Mi-Ru Youn, Chen Yu Liu, Namika Takada (BU MSDS 2025)

Introduction

Boston‚Äôs public transportation has a rich history spanning nearly 400 years. Today, the MBTA serves over 1 million passengers and contributes an estimated $11.5 billion annually to the Greater Boston economy. This project delivers a detailed analysis of MBTA bus performance between 2019 and 2023, with a focus on ridership trends, travel times, delays, and equity impacts.

Data Collection and Cleaning

Data were sourced from the MBTA Open Data Portal, including ridership counts, bus arrival/departure times, and demographic surveys. Ridership calculations were based on automatic passenger counts and fare collection data. Data preprocessing included handling inconsistencies in boardings vs. alightings, missing values, and aligning scheduled vs. actual departure times to better capture waiting times and delays.

Conclusion

The MBTA bus system experienced significant ridership decline during the pandemic, with gradual recovery by 2023. However, service disparities remain: routes serving Environmental Justice communities tend to face longer delays and reliability issues. Addressing these disparities is essential to ensuring equitable access to public transit in Boston. Data-driven improvements such as increased service frequency and targeted scheduling can help reduce inequities and enhance transit reliability.
